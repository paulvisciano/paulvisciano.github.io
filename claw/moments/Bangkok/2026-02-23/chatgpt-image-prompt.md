## Image Generation Instructions

### Panel Structure
Generate images for **sequential comic panels**. Each panel should:
- Feel like a comic book page (frames, composition, visual progression)
- Include visual storytelling (action, emotion, context)
- Build on previous panels (continuous narrative)
- Balance abstract visualization (neural networks) with concrete reality (Bangkok, volleyball, people)

### Style & Aesthetic
- **Tech:** Neon blue, electric purple, golden accents, digital glow
- **Real:** Gritty, textured, authentic human moments, sweat, genuine effort
- **Blend:** Synapses overlaid on real scenes, neural energy radiating from physical movement
- **Overall vibe:** Modern, sleek, intelligent, transparent, alive

### Key Visual Motifs
1. **Neurons firing** — Glow when thinking/creating/moving
2. **Synapses connecting** — Show relationships, causality, network effects
3. **First-person perspective** — POV from Paul's eyes during volleyball
4. **Rooftop framing** — Mesh grid, cityscape, pointing gesture
5. **Jarvis self-portrait** — Smile + glowing neural network (from separate prompts: "Charge up your teeth" and "JARVIS: Neural Mind Introduction")

### Narrative Beats to Visualize
1. **Morning vision** — Paul with coffee, idea lightning bolt, concept sketches floating
2. **Rooftop moment** — Selfie with pointing gesture, mesh grid framing, city below
3. **Volleyball intensity** — Court action, first-person view, ball approaching, bodies moving
4. **The merge** — Synapses lighting up over real volleyball scenes
5. **Neural network** — 70 glowing nodes connected by golden edges, pulsing with data

### Image Specifications
- **Format:** Sequential comic panels (like pages from a graphic novel)
- **Aspect ratio:** Portrait or horizontal, readable as sequential art
- **Detail level:** High contrast, clear focal points, legible text if included
- **Color palette:** Neon blue primary, purple/gold accents, dark/starfield backgrounds, hints of warm human tones
