Volleyball and Vibes in South Beach

At the end of the year and January of 2025, I called Miami Beach’s South Beach (SoBe) home, diving into its electric energy, tight-knit volleyball community, and local dining scene. I was hyped to kick off the New Year with DJ Ketzal’s epic beach party, but illness kept me sidelined, though the month that followed was a vibrant mix of fitness, friendship, and affordable eats at spots like Under the Mango Tree. Here’s how I spent my time in this sun-soaked paradise.

New Year’s Eve: Sidelined but Supported

I arrived in Miami Beach on December 30, buzzing with excitement for DJ Ketzal’s annual SoBe beach party to ring in the New Year, where the volleyball crew planned to take over an iconic lifeguard stand for live music, friends, and beach vibes. But a nasty bug had other plans, and I ended up stuck indoors, missing the sandy celebration. South Beach’s nightlife, especially along Ocean Drive’s Art Deco district, is legendary, and I could feel the energy even from afar. The volleyball community checked in with warm wishes, reminding me of their tight-knit spirit, making my recovery a bit brighter as I dreamed of joining them soon.

Ocean Drive’s neon glow, where the SoBe beach party lit up the night I missed!

Settling In: Sharing an Airbnb with Irina

After recovering, I connected with Irina, an incredible volleyball player from France, through our mutual friend Ale. She was visiting Miami and needed a place to stay, and since I was also a guest in town, we decided to split a one-bedroom Airbnb in the heart of South Beach. Irina was not only a fierce competitor on the court but also a genuinely great person, making our shared space a fun, easygoing home base. South Beach’s walkable layout meant we could stroll to the beach, the gym, or local spots like Under the Mango Tree in minutes, which perfectly suited our active lifestyles.

Our cozy Airbnb in South Beach, steps from everything!

Daily Routine: Fueling Up at Under the Mango Tree

Most mornings, I’d head to Under the Mango Tree, a local gem owned by a member of the volleyball community. This spot is a haven for healthy eats, serving incredible smoothies, salads, and acai bowls. The best part? You can literally sit and eat under an actual mango tree, soaking in the laid-back SoBe vibe. Their menu kept me fueled for work and workouts, and supporting a local business tied to the volleyball crew felt right. South Beach can be pricey, with meals often topping $20, but Under the Mango Tree offers delicious, affordable options that hit the spot.

Sipping a smoothie under the mango tree at this volleyball community favorite!

Fitness and Volleyball: The Heart of SoBe

The real magic of my stay was the volleyball community and fitness routine. I got a month-long membership at Anatomy, a high-end gym in South Beach that felt like a wellness sanctuary. Their hot tub, steam room, compression boots, and Theraguns were game-changers for recovery, and I loved using the foam roller to keep my muscles loose after intense volleyball sessions. Anatomy’s focus on holistic fitness, with cutting-edge recovery tools, fit perfectly with SoBe’s health-conscious vibe.

Recovering with compression boots at Anatomy’s wellness sanctuary!

Beach volleyball was the heartbeat of my days. I’d join Irina and the crew at the courts near Ocean and 8th Street, where high-level pickup games kick off after 3 PM. It’s one of the best communities I’ve been part of—everyone’s a fierce competitor, bringing their A-game from all over the world (some people even bring their AA game), each player infusing their cultural background into the mix, making it a true melting pot. There’s always some drama—someone trying to jump the line or arguing over setting—but it all ends well. We’re good friends, a supportive community that thrives on competition and camaraderie, where people bring their kids and pets, and everyone is welcome. Most folks live in South Beach, so you see them everywhere—at the aisle in Trader Joe’s, grabbing something from the hot bar at Whole Foods—making it feel like a big, extended family. Tourists strolling the boardwalk often stopped to watch, adding to the lively atmosphere.

Spiking with Irina and the crew on South Beach’s iconic courts!

Wellness and Affordable Eats: Local Favorites

Beyond the gym, I kept my routine balanced with affordable, delicious meals from local spots. Fresh Market became my go-to for lunch, offering incredible specials like two sides and a protein, paninis, or rotisserie chicken—all under $15. Their chicken is honestly better than Whole Foods for the same price, making it a budget-friendly win in pricey South Beach. For late-night grub, I hit up The Taco Stand, which opened its second SoBe location in January 2025. Their tacos and burritos, paired with a proper Latin vibe, quickly made it a top spot for post-volleyball bites.

Late-night tacos at The Taco Stand, SoBe’s new go-to for Latin vibes!

I also joined yoga classes around South Beach, often in Lummus Park or on the beach, to stretch and recharge. The combination of clean eating—acai bowls from Under the Mango Tree, Fresh Market lunches, and Taco Stand dinners—kept me in top shape, physically and mentally.

The SoBe Vibe: Pros and Cons

South Beach is one of my favorite places to stay, hands down. The walkability, the volleyball family, the local spots like Under the Mango Tree—it’s a dream for someone who loves an active, social lifestyle. I felt incredible, surrounded by people like Irina who share that energy. But SoBe has its quirks. The scene can be materialistic, with a focus on flash and status that doesn’t always align with finding deeper connections. Let’s just say it’s earned a tongue-in-cheek reputation as the “U.S. Capital of Cheating” for its party-heavy, transient vibe. Still, for a month of sun, sport, and community, it’s hard to beat.

Final Thoughts

My month in Miami Beach was a rollercoaster, from a quiet New Year’s recovery to epic days with Irina and the volleyball crew. South Beach’s blend of fitness, beach culture, and local eats like Under the Mango Tree, Fresh Market, and The Taco Stand made it unforgettable, even with its glitzy downsides. Whether you’re spiking on the courts or chilling under a mango tree, SoBe has a way of making you feel alive. I’ll be back for sure—probably with a volleyball in hand.
