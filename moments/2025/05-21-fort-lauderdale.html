Volleyball, Vibes, and Relaxation in Fort Lauderdale

May 21–23, 2025

Late Night Arrival and Creative Flow

Touching down in Fort Lauderdale was the start of a whirlwind adventure. My buddy Bartek picked me up from the airport, and we headed straight to his place. A coffee buzz from the flight kept me wired, so I ended up tweaking my travel blog until 3 a.m.—nothing like a late-night creative session to kick things off.

Work and Local Food Discoveries

Morning brought work meetings for my new role at OutSystems, which I started this week. Working remotely from Bartek's apartment was seamless, but the real gem was lunch at Broward Meat and Fish Market. This place is a food lover's paradise, with a deli counter boasting 8–10 kinds of meats, fresh salads, and colorful veggies, all within a sprawling supermarket. We grabbed a haul of deliciousness, and I was blown away by the selection.

Beach Volleyball and Relaxation

After a quick nap and more work, we headed to Deerfield Beach for volleyball. The warm-up got us pumped, the games were intense, and the ocean was just the right temperature for a refreshing swim to cool off. A storm threatened but passed us by, letting us play on. Afterward, Bartek and I treated ourselves to a relaxing massage together—a perfect way to unwind after spiking and diving. We polished off our deli goodies and stayed up late catching up, those heart-to-heart chats making the trip unforgettable.

Wrapping Up and Moving On

This morning, I tackled more work meetings and squeezed in a quick workout. Now, I'm waiting for an Enterprise rental to drive to Miami International Airport for my Global Entry renewal—hoping it's a breeze. Fort Lauderdale served up the ultimate mix of work, play, and relaxation, and I'm already itching to return.

Pro Tips

Hit Deerfield Beach for volleyball in the late afternoon—games are lively, and the ocean is your cooldown spot. Don't miss Broward Meat and Fish Market for a lunch that'll leave you spoiled for choice, and book a massage to cap off an active day.
