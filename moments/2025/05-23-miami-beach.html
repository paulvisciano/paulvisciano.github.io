South Beach Volleyball, Bachata, and a Drive to St. Pete

Date: May 23–30, 2025

Hey, fellow travel enthusiasts! I just spent a week soaking up the electric energy of South Beach, Miami Beach, and it was a whirlwind of sun, sand, and unforgettable moments. From spiking volleyballs with friends to learning new bachata moves at Alain's apartment and driving a rental car to St. Petersburg, here's the full scoop on my vibrant stay in this iconic destination, complete with workouts, smoothies, and late-night tacos.

Volleyball Marathon and Knee Drama

South Beach is a beach volleyball mecca, and I dove right into the action at the courts on Ocean Drive and 8th Street. For four straight days, I was spiking, diving, and serving alongside my buddies Wouter, Danie, and local players. The vibe was pure adrenaline, and I even experimented with kratom from Purple Lotus, which gave me the boost to power through. But by day four, my knees were swollen and begging for a break. I didn't get as many games in as I'd hoped, but the quality of play was top-notch.

Bachata Nights at Alain's

When the sun set, the energy shifted to dance floors. I hit up Alain's for bachata, where the DJ spun a perfect mix of classics and modern hits. The crowd was welcoming, and I found myself dancing with both locals and tourists, each bringing their own style to the floor. If you're into Latin dance, Miami Beach has plenty of spots—check out Salsa Mia for a similar experience. It's a perfect way to dive into the city's vibrant culture and connect with new friends.

Living the South Beach Life

Between volleyball and dancing, I kept a solid routine: morning workouts at Muscle Beach to pump some iron under the sun, followed by beach time to catch rays. For fuel, we hit Under the Mango Tree and Pura Vida for refreshing smoothies, and started our mornings with coffee from Joe & The Juice, Umbria, or Panther Coffee. Late-night cravings? We'd head to The Taco Stand for killer tacos or Sandwicherie for their epic sandwiches. Every spot added to the South Beach vibe—colorful, lively, and full of flavor.

Rental Car Adventure to St. Pete

To head to St. Petersburg for the next leg of my journey, I rented a car one morning, expecting a standard SUV for the 260-mile drive. Plot twist—the rental agency handed me a compact Mitsubishi instead. Not the spacious ride I had in mind, but it was zippy enough to cruise up I-75 from Miami to St. Pete in about four hours. The compact size made navigating Miami's busy streets a breeze, and the open road offered a chance to soak in Florida's coastal vibes. Pro tip: double-check your rental car details, especially when planning a road trip!

Fresh Market Feasts

To fuel my recovery and adventures, I hit the South Beach Fresh Market almost daily. Their lunch special is a steal—a massive panini with six varieties to choose from. I'm obsessed with the Italian panini, loaded with fresh mozzarella, tomatoes, and basil. It's the perfect fuel for beach volleyball and dancing.

Why South Beach Steals the Show

Miami Beach is more than a shoreline—it's a lifestyle. The Art Deco Historic District dazzles with pastel-colored buildings and neon lights that scream retro glamour. At night, Ocean Drive pulses with nightlife, from bars to clubs spilling music into the streets. Beyond volleyball and dance, South Beach offers endless experiences—grab a healthy bite at Pura Vida, indulge at Joe's Stone Crab, or explore the Lincoln Road Mall. Whether you're into fitness, food, or just vibing, this place has it all.
