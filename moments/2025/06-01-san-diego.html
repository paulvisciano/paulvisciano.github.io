San Diego Coastal Reset: Remote Work and Beach Volleyball

June 1-13, 2025

Finding My Rhythm in San Diego

These two weeks in San Diego have been all about establishing a perfect balance between work, wellness, and play. Despite the surprisingly persistent gray skies—with only two truly sunny days during my stay—I've fallen into a productive routine that makes the most of both my European work schedule and San Diego's coastal lifestyle. The locals assured me that this gloomy June weather, known as "June Gloom," is actually quite typical for this time of year. What's truly impressive is how the San Diego community embraces beach life regardless of the weather—the beaches remain active and vibrant even under gray skies, showing just how deeply ingrained beach culture is here. While unexpected for visitors expecting endless California sunshine, it hasn't dampened the city's vibrant energy or outdoor activities.

Base in Chula Vista

I've made my home base in Chula Vista, in the southern part of San Diego County. What's impressed me most is San Diego's incredible highway system—despite being based in the south, getting around has been remarkably easy and quick. The efficient network of highways has made it seamless to maintain my routine, whether I'm heading to South Mission for volleyball, exploring different parts of the city, or meeting up with friends. This accessibility has been key to making the most of everything San Diego has to offer.

Early Morning European Sync

My days start early, syncing up with my team in Portugal. The time difference actually works in my favor—while San Diego is still waking up, I'm already deep in collaboration with my colleagues across the Atlantic. These morning meetings set a productive tone for the day and keep me connected with the team despite the physical distance.

Friends & Adventures

Reconnecting with Wouter has been a highlight of my San Diego stay. He's like a little brother to me—we've shared countless adventures across the globe, from the vibrant streets of Colombia to the historic charm of Cuba, from the beer gardens of Germany to the modern marvels of Dubai and Abu Dhabi. Having him here in San Diego has felt like another chapter in our ongoing travel saga. Meeting his girlfriend Ray has been wonderful; she's an incredible person who fits perfectly into our dynamic. Together, we've created new memories across the city—from late-night sessions at the hookah lounges to exploring various bars and restaurants around town. Their presence has made San Diego feel instantly familiar, adding that special warmth that only close friendships can bring.

Taking a break from the routine, I joined Wouter and Ray for a whale watching expedition off the San Diego coast. While the whales proved elusive that day, we were treated to an incredible show of dolphins swimming and playing in the waters around our boat. The cloudy skies and ocean spray couldn't dampen our spirits as we celebrated each dolphin sighting, a perfect example of how San Diego mixes work and play so effortlessly. Having friends like Wouter and Ray here has made the transition between cities smoother and more enjoyable.

Wellness and Workout Flow

After wrapping up morning meetings, I kick off my wellness routine with a refreshing smoothie from Fresheria—the perfect fuel for what's ahead. Then it's straight to the gym for a solid training session, building strength and maintaining fitness for beach volleyball. The consistency of this morning routine has been key to staying energized and focused.

South Mission Beach Volleyball

The highlight of each day is hitting the sand at South Mission Beach for volleyball. The courts here have become my second home, offering both great competition and a chance to soak in that California sunshine. What makes this spot special is the vibrant volleyball community—you can find fun pickup games pretty much any day of the week at the main courts. For those seeking more competitive play, there's a smaller private group that meets further up the beach, offering a higher level of play and more structured matches. Playing alongside Wouter, we've managed to surprise some of the local favorites with our dynamic teamwork, adding our own mark to the competitive scene here. The beach volleyball community here is strong, and the level of play keeps me challenged and improving.

Recovery and Remote Work

Post-volleyball, I either head back to the gym for a refreshing shower or treat myself to a massage—essential for recovery and maintaining peak performance. The afternoons and evenings find me working remotely from various spots around town, with Tabac becoming my primary hookah lounge workspace.

Tabac: Work and Ambience

Tabac has become my go-to spot for combining work with a relaxed atmosphere. The lounge nails the ambience with its perfectly curated playlist, mixing Middle Eastern and electronic music that sets just the right mood. Their extensive hookah menu offers countless flavor options and different types of hookahs to choose from. The food has been a highlight too—I've become a regular fan of their hummus and gyro combination. The only minor drawback is the low tables, which can get a bit uncomfortable during longer work sessions. Despite this, the overall vibe and quality keep me coming back.

For those seeking more of a restaurant experience, Maestro offers an excellent alternative. With its more traditional dining setup, it's perfect for when you want to enjoy hookah in a full restaurant atmosphere.

Local Food Gems

One of my favorite discoveries has been Northgate Market, an incredible Mexican supermarket that's so much more than just a grocery store. Their hot food section is extensive and impressive, offering a wide variety of authentic Mexican dishes ready to eat. It's been a perfect spot for getting delicious, authentic meals while experiencing a slice of San Diego's vibrant Mexican food culture.

Coffee Shop Finds

One of my favorite discoveries has been Cafe Tropics. This gem has become my go-to spot, serving up amazing acai bowls and perfectly crafted espresso drinks. What makes it even better as a remote work location is its abundance of seating, reliable WiFi, and plenty of outlets—everything a digital nomad needs. The combination of great food, excellent coffee, and work-friendly amenities makes it an ideal spot for either focusing on tasks or taking a refreshing break between activities.

For those pre-volleyball caffeine fixes, Blue Palm has been a game-changer. Strategically located near the South Mission courts, it's perfect for grabbing a quick espresso before hitting the sand. The convenient location has made it an essential part of my volleyball routine.

When staying closer to my Chula Vista base, Home Coffee has been my local go-to spot. It's the perfect option for those days when I want quality coffee without venturing too far from home.

Why This Routine Works

This San Diego routine has proven to be incredibly effective, combining productivity with active living. The early start with European meetings allows me to front-load my workday, leaving plenty of time for fitness and volleyball. The city's perfect weather and outdoor lifestyle make it easy to maintain this balanced schedule, while the various remote work spots keep things interesting and prevent monotony.

Looking Ahead

As my time in San Diego draws to a close, I'm grateful for these two weeks of structured living and meaningful connections. Tonight's farewell Mexican dinner at a highly recommended local spot with Wouter and Ray will be the perfect way to cap off this chapter. This routine has set a great template for maintaining work-life balance, something I'm looking forward to adapting to my next chapter in Hawaii.
